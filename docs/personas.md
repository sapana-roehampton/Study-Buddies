# Personas

## Persona 1: Isha – First-Year Student Seeking Academic Support

**Age:** 19  
**Course:** BSc Computer Science  
**Year:** First Year  
**Technical Skill:** Beginner programmer  
**Devices Used:** Laptop and smartphone  

### Background
Isha recently started university and is adjusting to independent study. She finds programming modules challenging and feels overwhelmed by the pace of lectures. She is motivated to improve but lacks confidence in asking questions publicly.

### Goals
- Understand difficult programming concepts  
- Prepare effectively for exams and coursework  
- Build friendships within her course  
- Gain confidence through collaborative learning  

### Frustrations
- Feels uncomfortable asking questions in large lectures  
- Does not know who else is studying the same modules  
- Often studies alone  
- Finds it difficult to organise study sessions manually  

### Behaviour
- Uses messaging apps but rarely posts academic questions  
- Searches online tutorials when confused  
- Studies more effectively in small groups  

### Scenario
Midway through the semester, Isha is preparing for a Web Development coursework submission. She does not fully understand Express routing and feels stressed. She logs into Study Buddies, selects her module, and searches for students studying the same subject. She finds two students available and sends a request to arrange a study session.  

Through the platform, she organises a library session. After the discussion, she feels more confident and better prepared to complete her coursework.

### Success Criteria
- She can easily find suitable study partners  
- Study sessions are simple to arrange  
- She feels more confident after collaboration

## Persona 2: Abishek – Second-Year Student Offering Academic Support

**Age:** 22  
**Course:** BSc Computer Science  
**Year:** Second Year  
**Technical Skill:** Intermediate/Advanced  
**Devices Used:** Laptop  

### Background
Abishek performed well in his first year and is confident in subjects such as Databases and Web Development. He wants to strengthen his CV by mentoring junior students and contributing positively to the university community.

### Goals
- Support first-year students  
- Reinforce his own knowledge  
- Gain mentoring and leadership experience  
- Contribute to a collaborative learning environment  

### Frustrations
- No structured system to connect with students who need help  
- Informal group chats are disorganised  
- Does not want long-term tutoring commitments  

### Behaviour
- Actively participates in academic discussions  
- Shares helpful resources with peers  
- Prefers structured and scheduled sessions  

### Scenario
Before exam season, Abishek has free evenings and wants to help students struggling with Databases. He logs into Study Buddies and lists the subjects he can support, along with his availability. He receives a study request from two first-year students.  

He accepts the request, arranges a session, and after the meeting receives positive feedback through the platform. His profile reflects his contribution, increasing his credibility within the community.

### Success Criteria
- He can easily connect with students who need help  
- Sessions are scheduled efficiently  
- He receives recognition or feedback for his contribution
